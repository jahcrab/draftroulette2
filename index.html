Draft Roulette

Seven spins · seven seasons · one lineup

The wheel lands on a real NFL team-season. Sign one player from it and you get that player's actual PPR season — every point he really scored. Do it seven times and you have a lineup. The catch is the draft board: each player still costs the round he went in that year, and you only get one slot per round.

The round rule
One player per round

A player costs his ADP round that season. Take a 1st-rounder and you can't take another. Later picks can move up into an open earlier round — never down.

Free agents
Undrafted = wild card

Nobody drafted him that August, so he fits any open round. The waiver-wire league winners live here.

Scoring
Full PPR, full season

Total points, so games missed hurt. Your score becomes a record against everyone else's.

Normal mode shows each player's finish before you pick. Hard mode hides it — you draft on the name, the year, and the round, the way you would have in August.
